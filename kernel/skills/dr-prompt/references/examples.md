## 1. Technical Analysis

**Use for:** Engineering deep-dives, technology comparisons, R&D research

```
<RESEARCH_PROMPT>

## OBJECTIVE
To conduct a comprehensive technical review of current hydrogen storage technologies, evaluating their advancements, efficiency, scalability, safety, and economic feasibility over the past three years (2022–2025). The research should focus on the latest developments, innovations, and emerging trends relevant to expert engineers in the field.

## SCOPE
- **Timeframe:** 2022–2025
- **Geography:** Global
- **Technologies Covered:**
  - Physical Storage (Compressed gas, cryogenic liquid storage)
  - Chemical Storage (Hydrides, ammonia, liquid organic hydrogen carriers - LOHC)
  - Materials-Based Storage (Metal-organic frameworks - MOFs, carbon-based materials, nanostructured materials)
  - Hybrid and Novel Storage Concepts
- **Key Industry Sectors:** Energy production, transportation, industrial applications

## KEY QUESTIONS

### Technological Overview
1. What are the main hydrogen storage technologies currently in use or under development?
2. What are their fundamental working principles, strengths, and limitations?

### Performance & Efficiency
3. How do these technologies compare in terms of energy density, efficiency, and loss rates?
4. What breakthroughs have improved efficiency or reduced losses?

### Economic and Industrial Viability
5. What are the cost implications of each storage method, including infrastructure and operational costs?
6. How does scalability affect deployment in industrial applications?

### Safety and Regulatory
7. What are the major safety risks associated with each storage technology?
8. What regulatory frameworks exist for hydrogen storage, and how do they vary globally?

### Future Outlook
9. What are the most promising research directions and experimental approaches?
10. How do emerging materials improve storage efficiency and safety?

## DEPTH REQUIREMENTS
- **Analysis level:** Expert-technical (engineering-level detail)
- **Required metrics:** Energy density, efficiency rates, cost per kg, safety indices
- **Comparative views:** Side-by-side technology comparison tables
- **Data granularity:** Include key equations, performance benchmarks
- **Visuals:** Comparison charts, performance matrices

## REPORT FORMAT
1. Executive Summary
2. Introduction & Background
3. Overview of Hydrogen Storage Technologies
4. Technical Analysis & Performance Comparisons
5. Economic Feasibility & Industrial Applications
6. Safety Considerations & Regulatory Landscape
7. Future Trends & Research Directions
8. Conclusion & Recommendations
9. References & Data Sources

## METHODOLOGICAL CONSIDERATIONS
- **Primary sources:** Peer-reviewed journals, conference proceedings, patents
- **Analytical frameworks:** Technical benchmarking, standardized efficiency metrics
- **Quality criteria:** Recency (2022+), reproducibility, peer review status
- **Handling conflicts:** Compare methodologies, note measurement differences

## ADDITIONAL GUIDELINES
- Synthesis over summary - provide critical analysis, not just description
- Balanced evaluation of strengths and weaknesses
- Prioritize well-documented technical sources
- Consider interdisciplinary impacts (materials science, mechanical engineering, energy economics)

## ASSUMPTIONS & UNCERTAINTIES
- Focus on technologies with commercial or near-commercial readiness
- Exclude purely theoretical concepts without experimental validation

</RESEARCH_PROMPT>
```

## 2. Company Profiling

**Use for:** Competitor analysis, market entry research, partnership due diligence

```
<RESEARCH_PROMPT>

## OBJECTIVE
Develop a comprehensive company profile of ochutnejorech.cz (Czech e-shop specializing in nuts, dried fruits, and healthy snacks) to provide deep context for AI-driven marketing and business analysis. Map both visible customer-facing elements and underlying brand DNA.

## SCOPE
- **Timeframe:** Current state (2025)
- **Geography:** Czech Republic, Slovakia
- **Topical areas:**
  - Customer-visible elements (UX, offer, brand, messaging, customer journey)
  - Production, roasting, product processing
  - Product range, quality, packaging
  - Pricing and business model
  - Competition in nuts/dried fruit/healthy snacks market
  - Target customer segments
  - Brand values and DNA
  - Market position and differentiation

## KEY QUESTIONS

### Company Identity
1. How should the company be categorized (e-shop, manufacturer, family business, specialist retailer)?
2. What products do they offer and how do they differ from other sellers?

### Production & Products
3. How does their roasting, production, and product preparation work?
4. What is the declared quality level, freshness, ingredient sourcing?
5. What is the scope of private-label/house brand products?

### Customer Experience
6. How does the e-shop UX, navigation, category structure, visual style perform?
7. How are products presented (descriptions, benefits, storytelling)?
8. What loyalty/retention programs exist?

### Brand & Communication
9. What is the tone-of-voice, messaging themes, emotional elements?
10. What is the mission, vision, brand personality, characteristic DNA?

### Market Position
11. Who are direct competitors (Grizly, Svět oříšků, Oříškovna)?
12. Who are indirect competitors (supermarkets, Rohlík, Košík)?
13. What is their main USP and differentiation?

## DEPTH REQUIREMENTS
- **Analysis level:** Analytical with strategic synthesis
- **Required elements:** UX audit, pricing comparison, competitor mapping
- **Data granularity:** Specific examples from website, communications
- **Structure:** Clearly separate facts vs. inferences vs. strategic intent

## REPORT FORMAT
1. Executive Summary
2. Company Identity & Market Position
3. Brand DNA (tone-of-voice, values, communication)
4. Product Range & Own Production
5. Quality, Sourcing, Processing Methods
6. Pricing & Business Strategy
7. E-shop & Customer Experience
8. Marketing, Content, Social Media
9. Target Customer Segments
10. Competition (direct & indirect)
11. Differentiation & Competitive Advantages
12. Synthesis: Why the Brand Works

## METHODOLOGICAL CONSIDERATIONS
- **Primary sources:** Website analysis, product pages, social media, reviews
- **Analytical approach:** Benchmarking against competitors, customer perspective analysis
- **Quality criteria:** Observable data over assumptions
- **Handling conflicts:** Note where brand claims vs. customer reviews differ

## ADDITIONAL GUIDELINES
- Synthesis over description - not just "what is visible" but "what it means for the brand"
- Balanced view - include both strengths and weaknesses
- Customer optic - what motivates purchase, what creates value perception
- AI-ready output - structured for use in further analysis and content generation

## ASSUMPTIONS & UNCERTAINTIES
- Based on publicly available information only
- Internal company data not accessible

</RESEARCH_PROMPT>
```

## 3. Strategic Consulting

**Use for:** Implementation strategies, transformation projects, advisory work

```
<RESEARCH_PROMPT>

## OBJECTIVE
Develop a practical, techno-strategic AI strategy for a mid-sized agency (~50 employees) based in Czech Republic / Central Europe. Deliverables: strategic recommendations, step-by-step implementation manual, and workshop materials for internal AI adoption across operations, services, product teams, and client projects.

## SCOPE
- **Organization size:** ~50 people agency (mid-size company)
- **Geography:** Czech Republic and Central Europe; include EU comparisons
- **Time horizons:**
  - Short-term (0–6 months)
  - Medium-term (6–18 months)
  - Long-term (18–36 months)
- **Topics:** Governance & risk, data readiness, use-case identification, MLOps & deployment, security & compliance, talent & org change, process templates and checklists
- **Exclusions:** ROI calculations, financial modeling

## KEY QUESTIONS

### Quick Wins
1. What are the fastest "no-regret" actions for an agency this size? (specific steps, responsibilities, timing)

### Governance
2. How to create a governance framework suitable for mid-size company - roles, responsibilities, model and data risk management principles?

### Data Infrastructure
3. How to assess and adjust data infrastructure for repeatable, secure AI deployment (data quality, maturity, cataloging, anonymization)?

### Use-Case Identification
4. How to structurally identify and prioritize AI use-cases with clear implementation plans (impact/effort scoring templates)?

### MLOps
5. How to design MLOps procedures and CI/CD for models in agency environment (lightweight, maintainable for 50 people)?

### Anti-Patterns
6. What are common mistakes when introducing AI in agencies and how to avoid them (with practical examples)?

### Talent & Training
7. What internal competencies to build and how to plan training / talent acquisition (including external partners)?

### Workshop Design
8. How to prepare workshops: content, agenda, practical exercises, meeting templates for leadership and teams?

### Local References
9. Which local (CZ / Central Europe) case studies can be used as reference - what worked and why?

## DEPTH REQUIREMENTS
- **Analysis level:** Techno-strategic (management principles + practical technical details)
- **Practical outputs:** Checklists, templates (risk assessment, use-case canvas, deployment checklist), step-by-step actions (who-what-when), workshop slides
- **Evidence base:** Prioritize local experience, CZ/CE case studies; supplement with international best practices
- **Specificity:** Each step includes estimated effort (low/medium/high), responsible role, inputs, outputs, KPIs

## REPORT FORMAT (Three Artifacts)
1. **Strategic Report (PDF, 10-15 pages)** - Summary + roadmap (0-36 months)
2. **Practical Manual (Word/Google Doc)** - Checklists and templates:
   - Use-case canvas
   - Data readiness checklist
   - Model deployment checklist
   - AI governance charter template
   - Risk scorecard
3. **Workshop Kit (slides + exercises + facilitator notes)** - 90-120 min agenda for leadership + team exercises

Each artifact with clear "what to do now" (first 30 days) and "what to do next" sections.

## METHODOLOGICAL CONSIDERATIONS
- **Data collection:** 5-8 stakeholder interviews (leadership, tech lead, PM, client lead), process audit, tool inventory
- **Analytical tools:** Data & AI maturity model, use-case scoring matrix (impact vs. effort), model risk matrix (safety, privacy, reputation)
- **Case study selection:** Target mid-sized agencies in CZ/PL/SK/DE/Austria; include 3-5 successes and 1-2 anti-patterns
- **Delivery:** Ready-to-use templates (editable docs + slides)

## ADDITIONAL GUIDELINES
- Synthesis over summary - integrated insights and practical guidance over theoretical overview
- Evidence & local relevance - prioritize local examples, explicitly mark international sources
- Actionability - every step must be executable in agency context (clear role, time, tool)
- Balanced analysis - include limitations and implementation risks
- Ready-to-use deliverables - templates PM can deploy immediately

## ASSUMPTIONS & UNCERTAINTIES
- Agency has basic digital infrastructure in place
- Specific tech stack unknown - recommendations should be tool-agnostic where possible

</RESEARCH_PROMPT>
```

## 4. Sales Preparation

**Use for:** Pre-meeting research, prospect analysis, sales intelligence

```
<RESEARCH_PROMPT>

## OBJECTIVE
Prepare a strategic situational analysis of KV Elektro (B2B/B2C electrical installation materials and lighting retailer) as preparation for first sales meeting. Research should reveal current market position, digital and business health, competitive landscape, key industry trends, and potential weaknesses/opportunities for growth or partnership.

## SCOPE
- **Geography:** Czech Republic
- **Timeframe:** Current state + development over last 3-5 years
- **Topics:**
  - KV Elektro - company profile, business model, product and customer structure
  - Electrical retail industry (B2B/B2C segment)
  - Competition (Elfetex, Sonepar, ELMO, DEK, etc.)
  - Digital presence and reputation (web, e-shop, social media, employee activity)
  - Financial health, operational performance, growth signals
  - Internal and external bottlenecks (process, personnel, technology, market)
  - Improvement opportunities and innovation potential

## KEY QUESTIONS

### Company Profile
1. How does KV Elektro operate - history, size, organizational structure, business model?

### Market Position
2. How does it rank in Czech electrical/lighting products market? Who are key competitors and how do they differ?

### Digital Strategy
3. How does KV Elektro use digital channels (web, SEO, e-commerce, social media, online marketing)? What is the digital footprint of their employees (LinkedIn, etc.)?

### Financial Health
4. What are main economic indicators, growth trends, profitability, stability?

### Bottlenecks
5. What potential bottlenecks slow the company - logistics, sales, HR, innovation, digitalization, customer experience?

### Opportunities
6. What could move KV Elektro forward - improvement areas, value-add opportunities, trends to leverage?

### Industry Trends
7. What changes shape Czech electrical market (digitalization, e-commerce, automation, ESG, green tech)?

## DEPTH REQUIREMENTS
- **Analysis level:** Strategic overview + quantitative backing
- **Data sources:** Public registries, company databases, LinkedIn, SimilarWeb, reviews
- **Qualitative insight:** Reputation, communication style, company culture, customer/partner image
- **Opportunity identification:** Areas with improvement potential or business opportunity

## REPORT FORMAT
1. Executive Summary (1 page) - Key findings
2. KV Elektro Profile - History, model, position, strengths/weaknesses
3. Market Context & Competition - Comparative competitor analysis
4. Digital Presence - Web, SEO, social media, engagement
5. Financial Overview - Available numbers, trends, performance indicators
6. Operational & Strategic Challenges - Potential bottlenecks
7. Opportunities & Recommendations - What would move them forward
8. Sales Brief - Proposed business angle

## METHODOLOGICAL CONSIDERATIONS
- **Methods:** Desk research + digital footprint analysis
- **Sources:** Website, social media, LinkedIn, Heureka, Google Reviews, Justice.cz, Merk.cz
- **Competitive benchmarking:** Compare key parameters (offer, price, marketing, online performance, reputation)
- **Sentiment analysis:** Online presence evaluation
- **Process weakness identification:** Estimate based on public data and industry patterns

## ADDITIONAL GUIDELINES
- Synthesis over summary - connect market, company, and customer behavior insights
- Balanced analysis - weaknesses + strengths + opportunities
- Context emphasis - frame findings within Czech B2B/B2C electrical market
- Sales relevance - formulate every insight with potential business use in mind

## ASSUMPTIONS & UNCERTAINTIES
- Based on publicly available information
- Internal company data not accessible
- Some bottleneck assessment is inference from industry patterns

</RESEARCH_PROMPT>
```

## 5. Trend Forecasting

**Use for:** Market predictions, strategic planning, technology adoption roadmaps

```
<RESEARCH_PROMPT>

## OBJECTIVE
Develop a strategic, realistic analysis of AI-shopping technology evolution in e-commerce sector in Central and Southeast Europe with outlook to 2026 and beyond. Provide e-shop owners and management (especially in food and FMCG segment) with practical, predictive insights on:
- What changes AI agents, automated shopping, and voice/chat interfaces will bring
- What "being prepared" means for an e-shop (UX, data, catalog structure, integrations, marketing, customer care)
- What trends and scenarios to expect and prepare for during 2025

## SCOPE
- **Timeframe:** 2025–2027 (main focus on 2026)
- **Geography:** Czech Republic, Slovakia, Poland, Hungary, Romania
- **Segments:**
  - Primary: Food, healthy nutrition, FMCG
  - Secondary: Healthcare and general retail e-commerce
- **Company type:** E-shops with 100-200M CZK annual turnover, teams of 15-30 people

## KEY QUESTIONS

### Technology Context
1. What exactly does "AI shopping" mean in 2026? (agent-based purchasing, AI assistants, automated shopping lists, voice interfaces)
2. What technologies are rising - LLM-based agents, voice-to-cart, API interfaces for assistants, e-shop integration with third-party assistants (GPT, Alexa, Siri)?
3. What differences exist between chatbots, autonomous agents, and voice interfaces for shopping processes?

### E-shop Impact
4. What website, data structure, and product catalog adjustments are needed for seamless AI agent integration?
5. What changes in UX, customer care, logistics, or SEO/SEM are required?
6. What role will personalization, recommendation algorithms, and dynamic pricing play?

### Customer Behavior
7. How do customers respond to automated shopping possibilities?
8. How are expectations, trust, loyalty, and decision-making changing?
9. How does AI shopping affect conversion rates, interactions, item browsing?

### Security & Ethics
10. What are main security threats and how to manage them (fraudulent orders, payment data abuse, fake agents)?
11. How to transparently communicate to customers what AI does/doesn't do and how it protects their data?
12. What ethical issues (bias, accessibility, manipulation) need addressing?

### Regulatory Framework
13. What current and planned laws/regulations apply to AI in retail and e-commerce (GDPR, AI Act)?
14. How do approaches differ across region countries (CZ, SK, PL, HU, RO)?
15. What steps ensure legal compliance in AI shopping automation?

### Strategic Recommendations
16. What should a realistic 6-18 month e-shop preparation plan look like?
17. How to communicate with development partners - what to have designed, how to specify requirements?
18. How to factor AI shopping into company strategy, budgeting, and innovation?

### Scenarios & Predictions
19. What are possible AI shopping development scenarios to 2028 (slow adoption vs. explosive growth)?
20. What happens if an e-shop doesn't prepare for this trend?
21. Who will be market winners - and why?

## DEPTH REQUIREMENTS
- **Analysis level:** Detailed strategic with practical impact focus
- **Predictive models:** 2-3 development scenario variants
- **Required outputs:**
  - Change requirements table across company areas (UX, development, product, marketing, legal)
  - Trend map or timeline of key function adoption
- **Data granularity:** Specific technology examples, adoption metrics where available

## REPORT FORMAT
1. Executive Summary (high-level for CEO and investors)
2. AI Shopping Technology Overview
3. E-shop Impact - What Needs to Change
4. Customer Behavior & UX Changes
5. Security, Ethics, Trust
6. Regulatory Framework
7. Development Scenarios (2025-2028)
8. Strategic Planning Recommendations
9. Appendices - Readiness checklist, technology overview, glossary

## METHODOLOGICAL CONSIDERATIONS
- **Methods:** Synthesis of research, trend reports, market data, expert opinions
- **Sources:** EU documents, technology reports, consulting firms (McKinsey, Deloitte, Forrester)
- **Case studies and predictive models**
- **Authoritative, verified sources**

## ADDITIONAL GUIDELINES
- Practicality emphasis - research must support real decision-making
- Synthesis over overview - connect insights across areas
- Segment differentiation - FMCG vs. healthcare e-shops
- Highlight threats, risks, and opportunities
- Mark areas with highest uncertainty / fastest evolution

## ASSUMPTIONS & UNCERTAINTIES
- Technology adoption rates are estimates based on current trends
- Regional regulatory implementation timelines may shift
- Consumer behavior predictions based on early adopter markets

</RESEARCH_PROMPT>
```

## Key Patterns Across Examples

**Notice how each example adapts:**

1. **Questions scale with complexity** - Simple topics: 8-10 questions. Complex strategic: 15-20 questions
2. **Depth matches audience** - Technical research: equations, metrics. Strategic: frameworks, scenarios
3. **Format follows function** - Single report vs. multiple deliverables based on use case
4. **Methodology fits topic** - Academic sources for technical, business databases for company research
5. **Practical outputs for actionable research** - Checklists, templates, workshop materials when implementation matters
